# IRS Publication 1: Your Rights as a Taxpayer

> **Reference:** IRS Pub 1 — Your Rights as a Taxpayer  
> **Codified:** IRC §7803(a)(3) — Taxpayer Bill of Rights (TBOR)  
> **Adopted:** 2014 (TBOR formally adopted by IRS)  
> **Last Reviewed:** 2025

---

## Overview

The Taxpayer Bill of Rights (TBOR) is a foundational set of 10 rights that every taxpayer has when dealing with the IRS. In collection and debt resolution contexts, these rights are critical — they define what the IRS *can* and *cannot* do, and what taxpayers are entitled to at every stage.

**Key resource:** IRS Taxpayer Advocate Service — 1-877-777-4778

---

## The 10 Taxpayer Rights

---

### 1. The Right to Be Informed
> *Taxpayers have the right to know what they need to do to comply with the tax laws.*

**What it means:** The IRS must explain its processes clearly. Notices must state the reason for action, how to respond, and what happens if you don't.

**In collection context:**
- Every collection notice (CP14, CP501, LT11, etc.) must clearly state the amount owed, why it's owed, and taxpayer options
- IRS must tell you how interest and penalties are calculated
- If the IRS changes its position, they must explain why
- You have the right to a plain-language explanation before any levy

---

### 2. The Right to Quality Service
> *Taxpayers have the right to receive prompt, courteous, and professional assistance.*

**What it means:** IRS employees must treat taxpayers with respect. If service is substandard, taxpayers can complain.

**In collection context:**
- Revenue Officers must identify themselves and explain their purpose
- IRS Collection employees must be professional during negotiations
- If an IRS employee is abusive or unprofessional, you can request a supervisor or file a complaint
- Taxpayer Advocate Service exists to help when normal channels fail

---

### 3. The Right to Pay No More than the Correct Amount of Tax
> *Taxpayers have the right to pay only the amount of tax legally due, including interest and penalties, and to have their payments applied correctly.*

**What it means:** You don't owe more than what the law requires. Errors in IRS calculations can and should be challenged.

**In collection context:**
- Always verify the balance due on IRS Account Transcripts — IRS calculations can contain errors
- Penalty calculations can be wrong — always audit penalties on CP notices
- If the IRS misapplied a payment, you have the right to have it corrected (Form 3911 or direct contact)
- Statute of limitations on assessment: 3 years from filing (generally)

---

### 4. The Right to Challenge the IRS's Position and Be Heard
> *Taxpayers have the right to raise objections and provide additional documentation in response to formal IRS actions.*

**What it means:** You can dispute what the IRS says you owe. The IRS must consider your documentation and respond.

**In collection context:**
- You can dispute a balance due by providing evidence (canceled checks, corrected returns)
- CDP hearings provide a formal forum to challenge collection actions
- IRS Appeals is a separate, independent office — you can request Appeals consideration before Tax Court
- Submitting an Offer in Compromise invokes this right — IRS must review and respond

---

### 5. The Right to Appeal an IRS Decision in an Independent Forum
> *Taxpayers have the right to a fair and impartial administrative appeal of most IRS decisions.*

**What it means:** IRS Appeals is independent from Compliance/Collection. If you disagree with an IRS determination, you can escalate.

**In collection context:**
- After CDP hearing, you can petition **U.S. Tax Court** within 30 days of Appeals determination
- If OIC is rejected, you have **30 days** to appeal the rejection to IRS Appeals
- If installment agreement is rejected/terminated, you can appeal
- Collection Appeals Program (CAP) is available for fast-track resolution of certain issues
- Tax Court, U.S. District Court, and Court of Federal Claims are available forums

---

### 6. The Right to Finality
> *Taxpayers have the right to know the maximum amount of time they have to challenge the IRS's position, as well as the maximum amount of time the IRS has to audit a particular tax year or collect a tax debt.*

**What it means:** There are legal deadlines on IRS action. After those deadlines, the IRS loses its authority.

**In collection context:**
- **CSED (Collection Statute Expiration Date):** IRS has **10 years** from assessment to collect (IRC §6502)
- **Assessment statute:** IRS generally has **3 years** from filing to assess additional tax
- **Fraud/substantial understatement:** Assessment window extends to 6 years (25%+ omission) or unlimited (fraud)
- After CSED expires, the IRS **cannot levy, lien, or collect** — the debt is legally uncollectible
- > See `tax_knowledge/resolution/csed_deep_dive.md` for full CSED strategy

---

### 7. The Right to Privacy
> *Taxpayers have the right to expect that any IRS inquiry, examination, or enforcement action will comply with the law and be no more intrusive than necessary.*

**What it means:** IRS collection actions must be proportional. The IRS cannot harass or overreach.

**In collection context:**
- IRS must follow proper procedures before levying (LT11 required for most levies)
- Revenue Officers cannot enter your home or business without proper authority
- IRS must consider less intrusive alternatives before seizing property
- Levy on retirement accounts requires higher-level IRS approval
- Disproportionate collection actions can be challenged at CDP

---

### 8. The Right to Confidentiality
> *Taxpayers have the right to expect that any information they provide to the IRS will not be disclosed unless authorized by the taxpayer or by law.*

**What it means:** Your tax information is protected. IRS employees cannot share it improperly.

**In collection context:**
- Information disclosed on Form 433-A/B (financial statements for collection) is protected
- IRS cannot share your account details with unauthorized third parties
- Tax practitioners must have Form 2848 (Power of Attorney) or Form 8821 on file to receive your information
- Unauthorized disclosure by IRS employees is a federal crime (IRC §7213)

---

### 9. The Right to Retain Representation
> *Taxpayers have the right to retain an authorized representative of their choice to represent them in dealings with the IRS.*

**What it means:** You do not have to face the IRS alone. CPAs, enrolled agents, and attorneys can represent you.

**In collection context:**
- **Form 2848** — Power of Attorney. Authorizes a representative to speak and act for the taxpayer.
- Once Form 2848 is on file, the IRS must communicate with the representative, not the taxpayer
- Low-income taxpayers may qualify for free representation via **Low Income Taxpayer Clinics (LITCs)**
- IRS cannot refuse to work with an authorized representative
- The right to representation applies during audits, Appeals, CDP hearings, and all collection activity

**LITC Finder:** irs.gov/advocate/low-income-taxpayer-clinics

---

### 10. The Right to a Fair and Just Tax System
> *Taxpayers have the right to expect the tax system to consider facts and circumstances that might affect their underlying liabilities, ability to pay, or ability to provide information timely.*

**What it means:** The IRS must consider your full situation, not just the numbers. Hardship matters.

**In collection context:**
- The IRS must consider CNC (Currently Not Collectible) status when a taxpayer genuinely cannot pay
- Collection Financial Standards are used to measure ability to pay — these are minimum standards, not maximums
- Taxpayers facing genuine hardship can request Taxpayer Advocate intervention (Form 911)
- The IRS must weigh public policy when deciding on Effective Tax Administration OICs
- Abatement of penalties for reasonable cause is part of this right

---

## Taxpayer Advocate Service (TAS)

The TAS is an **independent organization within the IRS** that helps taxpayers when:
- You are experiencing economic harm or facing immediate financial threat due to IRS action
- You have tried to resolve your problem through normal IRS channels and failed
- You believe an IRS system or procedure isn't working as it should

**How to contact TAS:**
- Phone: **1-877-777-4778**
- File **Form 911** — Application for Taxpayer Assistance Order
- Find local TAS office: irs.gov/advocate

**TAS can issue Taxpayer Assistance Orders (TAOs)** to temporarily halt IRS collection while issues are resolved.
