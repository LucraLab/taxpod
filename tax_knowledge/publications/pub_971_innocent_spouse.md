# IRS Publication 971: Innocent Spouse Relief

> **Reference:** IRS Pub 971 — Innocent Spouse Relief  
> **Form:** Form 8857 — Request for Innocent Spouse Relief  
> **TaxPod Status:** ⚠️ OUT OF SCOPE for pipeline — awareness document only  
> **Last Reviewed:** 2025

---

## Overview

When a married couple files a joint tax return, both spouses are **jointly and severally liable** for the full tax owed — even if only one spouse earned the income or caused the understatement. Innocent Spouse Relief allows one spouse to be relieved of responsibility for taxes, interest, and penalties if certain conditions are met.

> **TaxPod Note:** The pipeline does not currently handle innocent spouse cases. If a client raises joint liability issues, document it and refer to a CPA or tax attorney. This file exists for agent awareness only.

---

## Three Types of Relief

### 1. Innocent Spouse Relief (Classic)
**What it covers:** Relief from additional tax owed due to erroneous items (understatements) attributable to the other spouse.

**Eligibility requirements (ALL must be met):**
- Filed a joint return with an understatement of tax
- The understatement is due to **erroneous items** of the other spouse
- You **did not know and had no reason to know** about the understatement when you signed the return
- It would be **unfair** to hold you liable given all facts and circumstances

**Key factors IRS considers:**
- Whether you received a significant benefit from the understatement
- Whether you were deserted, abused, or under duress when signing
- Whether you are divorced, separated, or estranged from the other spouse
- Your level of education and involvement in financial matters

---

### 2. Separation of Liability Relief
**What it covers:** Allocates the understatement of tax between you and your spouse/former spouse based on each person's items.

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Filed a joint return with an understatement
- You are **divorced**, **legally separated**, or have been living apart for at least 12 months
- You **did not have actual knowledge** of the erroneous items at the time of signing

**Key distinction from Classic:** You only need to show you didn't have *actual* knowledge (not constructive knowledge). More favorable standard.

**Limitation:** You remain liable for your allocated share of the understatement.

---

### 3. Equitable Relief
**What it covers:** A catch-all for cases that don't qualify for the first two types.

**Use when:**
- You don't qualify for Classic Innocent Spouse or Separation of Liability
- You have an **underpayment** (not just understatement) — e.g., you knew the tax was owed but didn't pay
- Applies to both understated and underpaid taxes

**IRS considers (Rev. Proc. 2013-34 factors):**
- Marital status (divorced/separated favored)
- Whether you will suffer economic hardship if not granted
- Whether you knew or had reason to know of the item or unpaid tax
- Whether there was abuse or financial control by the other spouse
- Whether you significantly benefited beyond normal support
- Whether you made a good faith effort to comply with tax laws
- Whether the other spouse has a legal obligation to pay

## Deadlines

| Relief Type | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| Classic Innocent Spouse | **2 years** from date IRS first attempted collection against you |
| Separation of Liability | **2 years** from date IRS first attempted collection against you |
| Equitable Relief | Generally **2 years** from first collection attempt; may be extended |

> **Warning:** The 2-year clock runs from IRS *collection activity*, not from when the return was filed.

---

## How to Apply

1. **File Form 8857** — Request for Innocent Spouse Relief
2. IRS will contact the other spouse (unless domestic violence concerns apply)
3. IRS reviews and issues a preliminary determination
4. **60 days** to appeal a preliminary denial
5. Can petition U.S. Tax Court if denied

**If there is a threat of abuse:** IRS has special procedures — taxpayer can request that IRS not contact the other spouse (Form 8857, Part IV).

---

## What Innocent Spouse Does NOT Cover

- Tax debt attributable to the requesting spouse's own income or items
- Employment taxes (payroll) — separate rules apply
- Business taxes — generally separate rules
- Cases where the requesting spouse had actual knowledge and significantly benefited
