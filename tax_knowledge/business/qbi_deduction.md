# Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction — Section 199A

**Primary IRS Reference:** Section 199A, Publication 535, Form 8995 / Form 8995-A

---

## Overview

The **QBI deduction** (Section 199A) allows eligible taxpayers to deduct up to **20% of qualified business income** from pass-through entities. It was introduced by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) and is currently scheduled to expire after **December 31, 2025** (absent Congressional extension).

**Who benefits most:** Sole proprietors, S-corp owners, and partners in profitable businesses who are below the income thresholds — or who have sufficient W-2 wages to maximize the deduction.

---

## Basic Calculation

```
QBI Deduction = Lesser of:
  (a) 20% × Qualified Business Income, OR
  (b) 20% × (Taxable Income − Net Capital Gains)
```

For most small businesses below the thresholds, the deduction is simply **20% of net business profit**.

**Example:**
- S-corp net income (K-1): $200,000
- Taxable income (after all deductions): $180,000
- QBI deduction: Lesser of 20% × $200,000 ($40,000) or 20% × $180,000 ($36,000)
- **Deduction: $36,000**

---

## Eligible Entities

The QBI deduction is available to:
- Sole proprietors (Schedule C income)
- Single-member LLCs (disregarded entities)
- S-corporation shareholders (on their K-1 share of income)
- Partners in partnerships (on their K-1 share)
- Certain trusts and estates

**Not eligible:**
- C-corporations (they have their own 21% flat rate)
- Wages from employment (W-2 income is not QBI)
- Capital gains
- Investment income (dividends, interest)

---

## Income Thresholds and Phase-Outs (2025 Tax Year)

| Filing Status | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Complete |
|---|---|---|
| Single | $197,300 | $247,300 |
| Married Filing Jointly | $394,600 | $494,600 |

### Below Threshold (Full Deduction Available)

If **taxable income** is below the threshold:
- The 20% QBI deduction is available in full
- No W-2 wage or property basis limitations apply
- Applies even if you have $0 W-2 wages

### Above Threshold — W-2 Wage Limitation Phases In

If taxable income **exceeds** the threshold, the deduction is limited to the **greater of:**

1. **50% of W-2 wages** paid by the business, OR
2. **25% of W-2 wages** + **2.5% of the unadjusted basis** of qualified property (depreciable assets)

The limitation phases in **linearly** between the lower and upper thresholds ($50,000 range for single, $100,000 for MFJ).

**Example (above threshold, $0 W-2 wages):**
- Net S-corp profit: $300,000
- No employees, owner took $0 salary (unrealistic, but illustrative)
- W-2 wage limit: 50% × $0 = $0
- QBI deduction: $0 (fully limited)

**Example (above threshold, with salary):**
- Net S-corp profit: $300,000
- Owner W-2 salary: $100,000
- W-2 wage limit: 50% × $100,000 = $50,000
- 20% QBI: 20% × $200,000 remaining K-1 = $40,000
- Deduction: Lesser of $40,000 or $50,000 → **$40,000** (W-2 limit is not binding here)

### Stacking with S-Corp

Paying a **reasonable W-2 salary** as an S-corp owner serves dual purposes:
1. Required to avoid IRS scrutiny on SE tax savings
2. Creates W-2 wages that increase the QBI wage limitation, potentially preserving the full deduction

## Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs)

Certain **professional service businesses** face additional restrictions once income exceeds the phase-out threshold.

### What Is an SSTB?

The following fields are SSTBs (Section 199A(d)(1)(A)):
- Health (physicians, dentists, nurses)
- Law (attorneys)
- Accounting / CPA services
- Actuarial science
- Performing arts
- Consulting
- Athletics
- Financial services (investment advisors, wealth managers)
- Brokerage services
- Any business where the principal asset is the "reputation or skill" of employees/owners

### SSTB Impact

- **Below income threshold:** SSTBs receive the full 20% QBI deduction — no penalty
- **Above income threshold:** The QBI deduction for SSTBs **phases out completely**
- At the upper threshold, SSTB owners get **$0 QBI deduction**, regardless of W-2 wages

### Tech/SaaS and SSTB Classification

**Technology and SaaS businesses are generally NOT SSTBs.**

- Software development, engineering, product companies → NOT SSTB
- AI/automation tools (like LucraLab products) → NOT SSTB
- **Exception:** If the business primarily provides professional consulting services (not software), it may be classified as an SSTB under "consulting"

> **Practical guidance:** A SaaS company selling subscriptions to a software product is NOT providing consulting services and is NOT an SSTB. This means the QBI deduction is available even at higher income levels (subject only to W-2 wage limitations, not the SSTB disallowance).

## Multiple Businesses — Aggregation Rules

Taxpayers with multiple qualified businesses can **aggregate** them for QBI purposes (under Reg. §1.199A-4) if:

- They have a common ownership interest (same or similar owners)
- They share employees, services, or facilities

Aggregation can be beneficial if one business has high W-2 wages and another has high QBI — the wage limitation is applied to the combined total.

|---|---|

| Above threshold, multiple businesses, SSTB issues, aggregation | **Form 8995-A** (detailed version) |
