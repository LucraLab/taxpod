# Section 179 and Bonus Depreciation

**Primary IRS References:** Section 179, Section 168(k), Publication 946, Form 4562

---

## Overview

Businesses can recover the cost of capital assets (equipment, software, vehicles) through depreciation over several years, OR elect to deduct some or all of the cost immediately using:

1. **Section 179** — Elective immediate expensing (limited to taxable income)
2. **Bonus Depreciation (Section 168(k))** — Additional first-year allowance (can create a loss)

These two methods are often used **in combination** to maximize first-year deductions.

---

## Section 179 — Elective Immediate Expensing

### What It Does

Section 179 allows businesses to **expense** (deduct in full immediately) the cost of qualifying property placed in service during the tax year, rather than depreciating it over multiple years.

### 2025 Limits

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Maximum Section 179 deduction | **$1,250,000** |
| Phase-out threshold (assets placed in service) | $3,130,000 |
| Phase-out: dollar-for-dollar above threshold | — |
| Dollar limit fully eliminated when assets exceed | $4,380,000 |

### 2024 Limits (for reference)

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Maximum Section 179 deduction | **$1,220,000** |
| Phase-out threshold | $3,050,000 |

### 2023 Limits (for reference)

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Maximum Section 179 deduction | $1,160,000 |
| Phase-out threshold | $2,890,000 |

> These limits are indexed annually for inflation.

### Qualifying Property for Section 179

- **Tangible personal property** used in business: machinery, equipment, computers, servers, office furniture
- **Off-the-shelf computer software** (not custom-developed)
- **Qualified improvement property (QIP)** — improvements to interior of commercial buildings
- **Listed property** (vehicles, cameras — subject to additional limits)
- **Vehicles:** Subject to luxury auto limits (Section 280F); larger SUVs/trucks may qualify for higher limits

### What Does NOT Qualify

- Land and buildings (real property) — cannot use Section 179 for the structure
- Property used outside the U.S.
- Property used for furnishing lodging (generally — residential rental property restrictions apply)
- Property purchased from related parties

### Section 179 Income Limitation — Cannot Create a Loss

**Critical rule:** The Section 179 deduction **cannot exceed the taxpayer's taxable income** from all active businesses.

- Cannot create a loss (unlike bonus depreciation)
- Excess Section 179 carries forward indefinitely to future years
- For S-corps: limitation applies at both the corporate level AND the shareholder level

**Example:**
- Business taxable income: $80,000
- Equipment purchased: $200,000
- Max Section 179: $80,000 (cannot exceed taxable income)
- Remaining $120,000: use bonus depreciation (no income limit) or MACRS depreciation

## Bonus Depreciation — Section 168(k)

### What It Does

Bonus depreciation (also called "additional first-year depreciation") allows an **immediate deduction for a percentage** of the cost of qualifying property placed in service during the year.

### Phase-Down Schedule (Post-TCJA)

| Tax Year | Bonus Rate |
|---|---|
| 2018–2022 | **100%** |
| 2023 | **80%** |
| **2024** | **60%** |
| **2025** | **40%** |
| 2026 | 20% |
| 2027+ | 0% (unless extended by Congress) |

> **Note:** Congress has periodically extended or modified bonus depreciation. Monitor legislative developments for potential restoration of 100% bonus.

### Qualifying Property for Bonus Depreciation

- **5-year MACRS property:** computers, tech equipment, autos, research equipment
- **7-year MACRS property:** office furniture, general business equipment
- **15-year MACRS property:** land improvements (fences, parking lots, landscaping)
- **Qualified improvement property (QIP):** 15-year, eligible for bonus
- New **and used** property — as long as the taxpayer has not previously used the property
- Property with a recovery period of 20 years or less

### What Does NOT Qualify for Bonus Depreciation

- Land and building structures (27.5-year residential, 39-year commercial)
- Property used predominantly outside the U.S.
- Certain public utility property
- Property for which you elected out of bonus depreciation

### Key Advantage: Can Create a Loss

Unlike Section 179, bonus depreciation **CAN create a tax loss**. This loss may then be:
- Carried back to prior years (if the CARES Act carryback rules apply, or state law permits)
- Carried forward to offset future taxable income

## Combined Strategy: Section 179 + Bonus Depreciation

**Optimal approach for maximizing first-year deductions:**

1. **Apply Section 179 first** — up to the business taxable income limit
2. **Apply bonus depreciation** to remaining asset cost — can push income to zero or below

**Example (2025 — 40% bonus depreciation):**

- Business taxable income before asset purchases: $150,000
- New equipment purchased: $300,000
- Step 1: Section 179 = $150,000 (reduces taxable income to $0)
- Step 2: Bonus depreciation on remaining $150,000: $150,000 × 40% = **$60,000** (creates $60,000 loss)
- Remaining basis after bonus: $90,000 depreciated over MACRS life (5 or 7 years)
- Net operating loss: $60,000 carried forward

## Standard MACRS Depreciation (No Elections)

If neither Section 179 nor bonus depreciation is elected, assets are depreciated under **MACRS** over their recovery periods.

| Asset Type | Recovery Period | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Computers, servers, tech equipment | 5 years | 200% declining balance |
| Automobiles | 5 years | 200% DB (luxury limits apply) |
| Office furniture and equipment | 7 years | 200% declining balance |
| Land improvements | 15 years | 150% declining balance |
| Qualified improvement property (QIP) | 15 years | 150% DB |
| Commercial real estate | 39 years | Straight-line |
| Residential rental property | 27.5 years | Straight-line |

**Half-year convention:** Personal property is generally treated as placed in service at the midpoint of the year (July 1 for calendar-year taxpayers), giving half a year of depreciation in year 1 and year of disposition.

**Mid-quarter convention:** If more than 40% of depreciable personal property is placed in service in the last quarter, the mid-quarter convention applies (reduces first-year deduction for late-year purchases).

## Vehicle Depreciation — Luxury Auto Limits (Section 280F)

**Passenger automobiles** are subject to annual depreciation limits:

| Year | 2024 Luxury Auto Limit (No Bonus) | 2024 With Bonus Depreciation |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | $12,400 | $20,400 |
| Year 2 | $19,800 | $19,800 |
| Year 3 | $11,900 | $11,900 |
| Year 4+ | $7,160/year | $7,160/year |

**Heavy SUVs (>6,000 lbs GVWR):** Subject to Section 179 limit of $31,300 (2025; was $28,900 in 2023), but NOT subject to the passenger auto luxury limits — can deduct much more in year 1.

## Form 4562 — Where Elections Are Made

All Section 179 and bonus depreciation elections are made on **Form 4562 (Depreciation and Amortization)**:

- **Part I:** Section 179 election (enter property, cost, elected deduction)
- **Part II:** Bonus depreciation (Section 168(k)) — listed separately
- **Part III:** MACRS depreciation for property placed in service in current year
- **Part V:** Listed property (vehicles and other listed property)

Form 4562 must be filed in the first year property is placed in service, or any year a Section 179 election is made.
