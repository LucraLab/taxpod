# Penalty Abatement Guide: FTA and Reasonable Cause

> **Scope:** First Time Abatement (FTA), Reasonable Cause, and Statutory Exception penalty relief  
> **Key phone:** 1-800-829-1040  
> **Last Reviewed:** 2025

---

## Overview

Penalty abatement reduces the total balance owed by removing IRS-assessed penalties. It does **not** reduce the underlying tax principal or accrued interest. However, since penalties can represent 25–50% of the total balance, abatement can be highly impactful.

**Three paths to penalty abatement:**
1. **First Time Abatement (FTA)** — Administrative waiver for first-time penalty; no hardship required
2. **Reasonable Cause** — Based on facts showing the taxpayer acted in good faith
3. **Statutory Exception** — Based on written advice from the IRS that the taxpayer relied upon

> **Always try FTA first.** It has no documentation burden and ~75% approval rate.

---

## Part 1: First Time Abatement (FTA)

### What Is FTA?

FTA is an **administrative penalty waiver** granted by the IRS to taxpayers with a clean compliance history. It requires no proof of hardship or extenuating circumstances — just a clean prior compliance record.

**Legal basis:** IRS Policy Statement 20-1 (formerly P-2-8), Revenue Procedure 84-35

---

### FTA Eligibility Checklist

All three conditions must be met:

- [ ] **Clean penalty history:** No penalties in the 3 tax years immediately preceding the year you're requesting abatement for
  - "No penalties" means no FTF, FTP, or FTD penalties were assessed in those 3 years
  - Penalties that were already abated don't count against you
  - Estimated tax penalties (§6654) do NOT disqualify you for FTA

- [ ] **Current filing compliance:** All required returns are filed (or extensions are on file)
  - All years must be filed — even years not in dispute
  - A delinquent return filed the same day as the FTA request typically still qualifies

- [ ] **Current payment compliance:** Any tax debt is paid, in an installment agreement, or has an OIC pending
  - Does not require the full balance to be paid — just that you have a plan

---

### What FTA Applies To

| Penalty Type | FTA Eligible? |
|-------------|--------------|
| Failure to File (FTF) — §6651(a)(1) | ✅ Yes |
| Failure to Pay (FTP) — §6651(a)(2) | ✅ Yes |
| Failure to Deposit (FTD) — §6656 (payroll) | ✅ Yes |
| Accuracy-Related Penalties — §6662 | ❌ No |
| Civil Fraud Penalty — §6663 | ❌ No |
| Estimated Tax Penalties — §6654/6655 | ❌ No |
| Information Return Penalties — §6721 | ❌ No |

> FTA applies to the **most common collection penalties** — FTF and FTP. This is where the money is.

---

### Calculating FTA Savings

#### Penalty Rates

| Penalty | Rate | Maximum |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Failure to File (FTF)** | 5% per month of unpaid tax | 25% of unpaid tax (5 months) |
| **Failure to Pay (FTP)** | 0.5% per month of unpaid tax | 25% of unpaid tax (50 months) |
| **FTF + FTP combined** | 5%/month net (FTF reduced) | FTF: 22.5%, FTP: 25% = 47.5% total |

> When both FTF and FTP apply simultaneously: FTF rate is reduced from 5% to 4.5%/month, so combined rate is still 5%/month. FTF maxes at 22.5% (4.5% × 5 months), FTP continues to 25%.

#### Example Calculations

**Scenario A: FTP Only (filed on time, didn't pay)**
- Tax owed: $10,000
- 12 months delinquent
- FTP rate: 0.5%/month × 12 = 6%
- **FTP penalty: $600**
- FTA saves: **$600**

**Scenario B: FTF + FTP (late filing AND late payment)**
- Tax owed: $10,000
- Filed 8 months late, never paid
- FTF (5 months at 4.5% + 0% after): $2,250 (max reached at 5 months)
- FTP (8 months at 0.5%): $400
- **Total penalties: $2,650**
- FTA saves: **$2,650**

**Scenario C: Large balance, both penalties maxed**
- Tax owed: $50,000
- Filed late, never paid, 5+ years
- FTF (maxed): $50,000 × 22.5% = $11,250
- FTP (maxed at 25%): $50,000 × 25% = $12,500
- **Total penalties: $23,750**
- FTA saves: **$23,750** on the $50K balance

### How to Request FTA

#### Option 1: Call the IRS (Fastest)

**Call:** 1-800-829-1040

**Script for calling IRS:**
> *"I'm calling to request First Time Penalty Abatement under Revenue Procedure 84-35 and IRS Policy Statement 20-1. My client [name, SSN/EIN] has a clean compliance history with no penalties in the three tax years prior to [tax year]. All required returns are filed and there is [a payment arrangement / full payment]. Can you remove the [Failure to File / Failure to Pay] penalties for [tax year]?"*

- IRS can grant FTA immediately over the phone in many cases
- Request a **penalty abatement transcript** to confirm the adjustment was made
- If denied over phone, escalate to supervisor or follow up in writing

#### Option 2: Written Request
- Respond in writing to the CP notice assessing the penalty
- Include: taxpayer name, SSN/EIN, tax year, penalty type, and FTA eligibility statement
- Cite: "Revenue Procedure 84-35" and "IRS Policy Statement 20-1"
- Allow 30–60 days for IRS response

#### Option 3: IRS Online Account
- For some balance-due notices, penalty abatement requests can be made online at irs.gov
- Less reliable than phone for immediate resolution

---

### FTA — Common Questions

**Q: Can FTA be requested before paying the balance?**  
A: Yes. FTA can be requested on existing unpaid balances. Payment does not need to be made first.

**Q: Can FTA apply to multiple years at once?**  
A: Only one year per request (the earliest penalty year). In a multi-year delinquency, abate the first year's penalty. The subsequent years may then qualify for FTA if the abated year counts as a "clean year."

**Q: What if the IRS denies FTA?**  
A: You can appeal within 60 days of a denial. Also consider requesting reasonable cause abatement as an alternative.

**Q: Does FTA apply to payroll taxes?**  
A: Yes — FTD (Failure to Deposit) penalties for payroll are FTA eligible.

## Part 2: Reasonable Cause Abatement

### What Is Reasonable Cause?

Reasonable cause relief is granted when the taxpayer can show they **exercised ordinary business care and prudence** but were unable to comply with tax obligations due to circumstances beyond their control.

**Legal basis:** IRC §6651(a) — "addition to tax shall not apply if failure is due to reasonable cause and not willful neglect"

---

### Qualifying Circumstances

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Illness or Incapacity** | Serious illness, hospitalization, mental incapacity of taxpayer or immediate family member |
| **Death** | Death of taxpayer's family member or close associate who handled tax matters |
| **Natural Disaster** | Fire, flood, hurricane, earthquake — if the taxpayer was directly affected |
| **Reliance on Tax Professional** | CPA or tax advisor failed to file or pay; taxpayer provided all needed information |
| **IRS Error or Delay** | IRS gave incorrect information that taxpayer relied on |
| **Inability to Obtain Records** | Records destroyed in disaster, or held by adverse party in litigation |
| **Financial Hardship (limited)** | Severe, temporary financial hardship — **harder to win** than other grounds |

---

### Reasonable Cause — Documentation Strategy

**General rule:** The more documentation, the better. IRS will consider the totality of evidence.

| Situation | Documentation to Gather |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Illness | Medical records, hospital discharge summaries, doctor letters, disability awards |
| Death | Death certificate, obituary, documentation of decedent's role in tax filing |
| Natural disaster | FEMA declaration, insurance claims, photos, newspaper reports |
| Reliance on advisor | Engagement letters, email correspondence, advisor's error acknowledgment |
| IRS error | Written IRS advice, call transcripts, prior correspondence |

**Key test the IRS applies:**
1. Did the taxpayer have ordinary business care and prudence?
2. Was the failure caused by circumstances beyond the taxpayer's control?
3. Was the taxpayer's reliance on others reasonable?

---

### How to Request Reasonable Cause Abatement

**Written request format:**
1. State the penalty amount and tax year at issue
2. Explain the specific facts and circumstances
3. Connect those facts to the specific penalty period
4. Attach all supporting documentation
5. Cite reasonable cause standard: "IRC §6651(a)" and request abatement

**Submit via:**
- Response to the CP notice (most common)
- Separate letter to the IRS Service Center address on the notice
- Through a representative with Form 2848 on file

## Part 3: Statutory Exception

### What Is It?
Penalty relief based on written advice received **from the IRS** that the taxpayer reasonably relied upon, and that advice was wrong.

### Requirements
- Taxpayer must have requested advice in writing
- IRS must have responded in writing
- Taxpayer must have reasonably relied on that advice
- The penalty must have resulted directly from following that advice

### When to Use
- Rare — most cases do not involve written IRS guidance
- Strongest form of abatement when it applies
- Document everything: original letter to IRS, IRS response, and how you followed it

---

## Strategy: Combining Abatement with Other Resolution

Penalty abatement can and should be combined with other resolution strategies:

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|---------|
| Entering an OIC | Abate penalties first to reduce RCP calculation baseline |
| Setting up IA | Abate penalties to reduce total balance before computing monthly payment |
| CNC status | Abate penalties to reduce total balance (though it won't be collected currently) |
| Balance near CSED expiry | Abate to reduce moral pressure; let CSED run |

> **Best practice:** Always attempt FTA before submitting an OIC. A successful FTA reduces the RCP because it lowers the total balance. That can reduce the minimum OIC offer amount.

## Quick Reference

### The FTA Call Script (Print This Out)

```
"I'm calling to request First Time Penalty Abatement under 
Revenue Procedure 84-35/IRS Policy Statement 20-1. 

My client [FULL NAME], SSN/EIN [XXXXXXXXX], has had no penalties 
assessed in the three tax years prior to [TAX YEAR]. 

All required returns are filed and the balance is [paid in full / 
subject to an installment agreement / subject to an OIC].

I'm requesting removal of the [Failure to File / Failure to Pay / 
Failure to Deposit] penalty for tax year [YEAR] in the amount of $[AMOUNT].

Can you process this abatement today?"
```

### FTA Checklist (Before Calling)
- [ ] Pulled IRS Account Transcript to confirm penalty amounts
- [ ] Verified no penalties in 3 prior years (check transcript)
- [ ] Confirmed all returns filed (check transcript for all modules)
- [ ] Confirmed balance is paid or in IA/OIC
- [ ] Identified exact penalty type (FTF, FTP, FTD)
- [ ] Have client's SSN/EIN ready
